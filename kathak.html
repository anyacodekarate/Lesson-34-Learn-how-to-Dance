kathak Mudras and Poses

Explore the gestures (Mudras) and poses (Asanas) of kathak dance.

Pataka

Pataka is a simple hand gesture representing a flag. It is used to depict various concepts like wind or celestial beings.

Tripataka

Tripataka is the gesture of a flag with three parts, often used in Bharatanatyam to represent the deities, fire, and other elements.

Alapadma

Alapadma hands, as described in the Natyashastra, represent a style of hand movements akin to Lavana Mukula hands. This specific hand gesture is often associated with divine or auspicious meanings and plays a significant role in the iconography of deities..

Chandrakala

Chandrakala Mudra is the fourteenth hand gesture of the 28 Single Hand Mudras (Asamyutta Hastas) as described in the Abhinaya Darpana.

Anjali

Kartari-Mukha

Adi Mudra

The Adi Mudra is the foundational stance for Bharatanatyam, symbolizing the beginning of the dance. The body posture is upright, with the arms positioned in a relaxed manner.

Bhramari

The Bhramari pose is a spinning movement, often representing a bee or revolving motion. The arms are raised and the body rotates gracefully.

Prenkhana

Prenkhana is one of the most popular and basic ‘Pada Bhedas’ or leg postures. Along with the hand movements, the posture is called the Mandala Bheda.

Vishnu Asana

Vishnu Asana is a pose representing Lord Vishnu. The dancer assumes a stance that conveys the powerful and graceful form of the deity.

Tribhanga

The Tribhanga pose involves three bends of the body: the neck, torso, and knee. It creates an elegant triangular shape and is a key feature in Bharatanatyam.

ekapada

When Eka Pada is performed in the static position, then the posture is Sthana, and when the Eka pada is executed taking a turn, then the posture is Bhramari.
