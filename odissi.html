Odissi Mudras and Poses

Explore the gestures (Mudras) and poses (Asanas) of Odissi dance.

Pataka

Pataka is a simple hand gesture representing a flag. It is used to depict various concepts like wind or celestial beings.

Tripataka

Tripataka is the gesture of a flag with three parts, often used in Bharatanatyam to represent the deities, fire, and other elements.

Mushti

Mushti mudra is a hand gesture and yoga pose that involves clenching your fingers into a fist with your thumb wrapped around the outside. The word "mushti" comes from Sanskrit and means "fist" or "closed hand".

Shikhara

n the Natyashastra, Shikhara is described as a hand gesture that features a raised thumb. This mudra is versatile and can be used to depict various actions, including throwing a javelin or engaging in the act of painting. It plays a significant role in classical dance and performance, symbolizing different activities and emotions through its expressive movements.

Katakamukha

Katakamukha Mudra (Crab Face): This mudra formed by folding the fingers is a symbol of aquatic life. It is used in dance sequences depicting stories related to rivers, oceans, or the divine play of water.
